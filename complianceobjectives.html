HIPAA Fundamentals

Understanding the Two Main Provisions
üìã What is Compliance?
Compliance in healthcare involves adhering to laws, regulations, and guidelines governing healthcare operations. It ensures patient safety, confidentiality, accurate billing, and prevents fraudulent activities.
üîí Title I: Insurance Reform
Focuses on health insurance coverage availability and continuity. Protects individuals with pre-existing conditions and ensures insurance portability between jobs.
üíª Title II: Administrative Simplification
Establishes national standards for electronic healthcare transactions. Includes Privacy Rule, Security Rule, and Enforcement Rule to protect patient information.
Feature Title I: Insurance Reform Title II: Administrative Simplification
Primary Focus Insurance coverage and portability Privacy and security of health information
Key Purpose Protect individuals with pre-existing conditions Establish national standards for electronic transactions
Main Components Insurance market reforms Privacy Rule, Security Rule, Enforcement Rule
Impact on Patients Continuity of insurance coverage Protection of personal health information

Privacy Rule & Protected Health Information

Understanding PHI, Disclosure, and Patient Rights
üìÑ Protected Health Information (PHI)
Any information in a medical record that can identify an individual, created, used, or disclosed during healthcare provision. Includes demographic data, medical history, test results, insurance information.
üîç Use vs. Disclosure
Use: Sharing PHI within the organization
Disclosure: Releasing PHI outside the organization
HIPAA requires minimum necessary use and disclosure
‚öñÔ∏è Privileged vs Nonprivileged
Privileged: Treatment-related information requiring authorization
Nonprivileged: Basic facts like name, address, insurance info
Different rules apply for disclosure
üìö Key Privacy Terms
Covered Entity
Health plans, clearinghouses, providers who transmit health information electronically
Business Associate
Person/entity performing functions involving PHI on behalf of covered entity
Authorization Form
Written permission for PHI use/disclosure beyond treatment, payment, operations
NPP
Notice of Privacy Practices - explains how PHI is used and patient rights

Security Safeguards & HITECH Act

Three Safeguard Categories and Enhanced Protections
1Ô∏è‚É£ Administrative Safeguards
Policies and procedures for security management, workforce training, information access management, security incident procedures, and contingency planning.
2Ô∏è‚É£ Physical Safeguards
Facility access controls, workstation security, device and media controls, and physical barriers to protect electronic systems and buildings.
3Ô∏è‚É£ Technical Safeguards
Access control systems, audit controls, integrity controls, person/entity authentication, and transmission security measures.
‚ö° HITECH Act Provisions
Part of American Recovery and Reinvestment Act. Expands HIPAA to business associates, increases penalties, requires breach notifications, promotes EHR adoption.
üìú HIPAA Omnibus Rule
Strengthens privacy/security protections, expands patient rights, increases penalties, and incorporates HITECH Act requirements into HIPAA regulations.

Fraud vs. Abuse Prevention

Detection, Prevention, and Compliance Programs
Aspect Fraud Abuse
Definition Intentional deception for unauthorized benefit Practices inconsistent with sound medical/business practices
Intent Clear intent to defraud May be unintentional or negligent
Examples Billing for services not rendered, upcoding, phantom bills Excessive charges, unnecessary services, poor documentation
Legal Consequences Criminal and civil penalties Civil penalties, corrective actions
