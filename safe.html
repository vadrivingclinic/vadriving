Distracted driving involves any activity that diverts attention from driving, including talking or texting on your phone, eating and drinking, talking to people in your vehicle, or fiddling with the stereo, entertainment, or navigation system.

üç∫ Drinking and Driving

Drinking and driving is extremely dangerous and illegal. Alcohol impairs your ability to drive, affecting your reaction time, coordination, and judgment.

üìä Fatal Statistics (NHTSA 2019)
‚Ä¢ 10,142 people died in alcohol-impaired driving crashes
‚Ä¢ Accounting for 28% of all traffic-related deaths in the U.S.
‚Ä¢ One alcohol-related death every 52 minutes
‚ö° Effects of Alcohol
  • Reduced coordination and impaired judgment
  • Slower reaction times - critical seconds lost
  • Decreased ability to make quick decisions
  • False sense of confidence and ability
  • Impaired vision and reduced concentration
‚úÖ Prevention Tips
  • Plan ahead for a designated driver if you plan to drink
  • Use public transportation, taxis, or ride-sharing services
  • Stay overnight if you are unable to find a safe ride home
  • Never accept a ride from someone who has been drinking
  • Be a responsible host - don't let friends drive impaired

üò† Aggressive Driving & Road Rage

Aggressive driving includes behaviors such as speeding, tailgating, weaving in and out of traffic, running red lights, and other dangerous maneuvers. Road rage is an extreme form that escalates to violent behavior.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Causes
  • Traffic congestion and delays
  • Running late and time pressure
  • Stress from work or personal issues
  • General disregard for other road users
  • Anonymity of being inside a vehicle
‚úÖ Prevention Strategies
  • Allow plenty of time for your journey
  • Stay calm and patient, even in heavy traffic
  • Follow traffic laws and maintain safe distances
  • Avoid engaging with aggressive drivers
  • Do not make eye contact or respond to provocation
  • Report aggressive driving to authorities if it escalates

üõ°Ô∏è Proactive Safe Driving Strategies

üåü Essential Safe Driving Principles

‚úÖ Stay Focused
  • Keep 100% of your attention on driving
  • Avoid using your phone or other distractions
  • Scan the road 10-15 seconds ahead of your vehicle
  • Check mirrors every 5-8 seconds
‚úÖ Stay Alert
  • Avoid driving when tired or fatigued
  • Take breaks every 2 hours on long trips
  • Never drive under the influence of substances
  • Be aware of changing road conditions
‚úÖ Follow the 2-Second Rule
  • Keep a safe following distance from the vehicle ahead
  • Minimum of 2 seconds in good conditions
  • Increase to 4+ seconds in rain, fog, or darkness
  • 6+ seconds in ice or snow conditions

üåô Night Driving Safety

Night driving presents unique challenges due to reduced visibility and increased likelihood of encountering impaired or fatigued drivers.

üìä Night Driving Statistics (National Safety Council)
‚Ä¢ Traffic crash death rates are three times higher at night
‚Ä¢ 50% of traffic deaths occur at night
‚Ä¢ Reduced visibility accounts for 90% of driver's reaction ability
‚úÖ Night Driving Tips
  • Use headlights correctly and ensure they're properly aimed
  • Reduce speed and increase following distances
  • Avoid looking directly at oncoming headlights to reduce glare
  • Keep windshield and mirrors clean for maximum visibility
  • Be extra cautious at intersections and pedestrian crossings

üö¶ Intersection Safety

Approaching intersections requires careful attention and adherence to traffic rules to prevent collisions. A significant number of traffic accidents occur at intersections.

‚úÖ Intersection Safety Tips
  • Controlled Intersections: Follow traffic lights and stop signs
  • Uncontrolled Intersections: Slow down and prepare to yield
  • Right-of-Way: Vehicle on left yields to vehicle on right
  • Roundabouts: Yield to traffic already in the circle
  • Always: Look left, right, then left again before proceeding
  • Never assume: Other drivers will follow the rules or signals
