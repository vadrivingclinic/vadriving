üìè Safe Following Distance

The 2-Second Rule
2 Seconds
Maintain a 2-second gap between your vehicle and the one ahead. This provides adequate reaction and stopping time.
Increase to: 4 seconds in rain ‚Ä¢ 6 seconds in snow/ice ‚Ä¢ 8 seconds in heavy fog
‚úÖ Measuring Following Distance
  • Pick a fixed object on the road ahead (sign, tree, bridge)
  • When vehicle ahead passes it, start counting: "one-thousand-one, one-thousand-two"
  • You should pass the same object after saying "two"
  • If you pass before "two", increase your following distance
  • Practice this technique regularly to develop proper spacing instinct

‚úÖ Smooth Deceleration Technique
  • Begin slowing 150-200 feet before intended stop point
  • Apply brakes gently and progressively
  • Release pressure slightly just before coming to complete stop
  • This prevents "nose diving" and provides smoother ride
  • Easier on passengers and vehicle components

‚ö° Emergency Stopping Techniques

üö® Threshold Braking Mastery
Threshold Braking: Apply maximum braking force WITHOUT locking wheels.
Modern ABS: Press brake pedal firmly and continuously. System prevents wheel lock.
Older Vehicles: Pump brakes rapidly if wheels begin to lock.
Steering: Maintain control while braking hard. Look where you want to go.
‚ö†Ô∏è Emergency Stop Sequence
  • Scan: Quickly check mirrors for escape options
  • Brake: Apply firm, steady pressure on brake pedal
  • Steer: Maintain straight path or steer around obstacle
  • Hold: Keep braking until vehicle stops completely
  • Secure: Apply parking brake, shift to Park (or gear)
