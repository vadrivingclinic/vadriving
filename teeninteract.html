üö∏ Why Teen Driving Laws Matter

  • Crash Statistics: Teens are 3x more likely to be in fatal crashes. 16-year-olds have the highest crash rate of any age group. Graduated licensing has reduced teen crash rates by 20-40%.
  • Passenger Risk: The risk of fatal crash increases exponentially with each teenage passenger. One passenger = 50% increase, two passengers = 100% increase, three or more = 300% increase.
  • Night Driving: 40% of teen fatal crashes occur between 9 PM and 6 AM. Virginia's curfew addresses this specific risk period.

üìã Parental Responsibilities & Best Practices

  • Supervised Practice: The 45-hour requirement (15 at night) is a minimum. Research shows 100+ hours of practice significantly reduces crash risk. Practice in various conditions: rain, night, highways, and heavy traffic.
  • Parent-Teen Agreement: Create a written driving contract covering curfews, passengers, destinations, and consequences for violations. Update it as teen progresses through licensing stages.
  • Technology Monitoring: Use apps and devices to monitor speed, location, and phone use. Discuss the data regularly as a learning tool, not just punishment.
