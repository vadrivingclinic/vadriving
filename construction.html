‚úÖ Best Practices for Safe Navigation

üëÅÔ∏è Stay Alert & Minimize Distractions

Focus Intensely: Construction zones demand 100% attention. Put away phones, avoid eating, and minimize conversations.

Scan Constantly: Look for workers, equipment movement, changing signs, and potential hazards in all directions.

Avoid "Highway Hypnosis": Actively engage with the changing environment to maintain situational awareness.

üö¶ Follow Posted Instructions

Obey All Signs: Temporary construction signs provide critical information about speed limits, lane closures, and detours.

Respect Flaggers: Flaggers have legal authority. Their signals override regular traffic control devices.

Reduce Speed: Posted speed limits account for reduced safety margins. Slower speeds save lives.

üöó Maintain Safe Following Distance

Increase Following Distance: Use the 4-second rule in construction zones (double the normal recommendation).

Watch for Sudden Stops: Construction zones often require unexpected stopping for equipment, workers, or lane changes.

Avoid Tailgating: Tailgating reduces reaction time and increases collision severity.

üéØ Essential Safety Principles
  • Plan Ahead: Check traffic reports and allow extra travel time
  • Merge Early: Merge at first opportunity when lanes are closing
  • Be Patient: Accept delays as necessary for safety
  • Stay in Lane: Avoid unnecessary lane changes within the work zone
  • Watch for Exits: Some construction zones may have temporary exits
