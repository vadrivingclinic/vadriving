History of Insurance in the United States

Historical Timeline

Health insurance in America evolved from earlier risk-sharing models. Understanding this history provides context for today's complex healthcare system.

Early Origins (18th-19th Century)

Health insurance in America evolved from earlier forms of insurance. Marine and fire insurance companies established in the 1700s created the foundation for risk-sharing models that would later be applied to health.

The First Health Insurance (1850)

The first known accident and health insurance policy in the U.S. was issued by the Franklin Health Assurance Company of Massachusetts in 1850. These early policies were limited and primarily covered accidents rather than illness.

20th Century Developments

In the early 1900s, the first group health insurance plans emerged. Baylor University Hospital's pre-paid plan in 1929, which later evolved into Blue Cross, marked a significant milestone. Blue Shield plans for physician services followed in the 1930s.

Employer-Based Insurance (Mid-20th Century)

During World War II, wage freezes led employers to offer health insurance as a fringe benefit to attract workers. This established the employer-based health insurance system that dominates the U.S. today.

Government Programs (1960s)

The creation of MedicareFederal health insurance program for people 65 or older, certain younger people with disabilities, and people with End-Stage Renal Disease and MedicaidJoint federal and state program that helps with medical costs for people with limited income and resources in 1965 represented a major expansion of government involvement in health insurance, providing coverage for elderly and low-income populations.

Modern Era (21st Century)

The Affordable Care Act (2010) brought the most significant reforms since Medicare/Medicaid, expanding coverage, regulating insurance practices, and creating health insurance marketplaces.
