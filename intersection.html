‚úÖ 7-Step Intersection Approach

üéØ Systematic Intersection Approach
  • 1. Scan Ahead: Look 12-15 seconds ahead for upcoming intersections
  • 2. Reduce Speed: Begin slowing down 150-200 feet before intersection
  • 3. Check Controls: Identify traffic signals, signs, and lane markings
  • 4. Look Left-Right-Left: Perform systematic visual scan before entering
  • 5. Signal Intentions: Use turn signals 100 feet before intersection
  • 6. Clear Path: Ensure your path is clear of all hazards
  • 7. Proceed Smoothly: Maintain steady speed without sudden movements

üö¶ Controlled Intersection Protocols

‚ö†Ô∏è Traffic Light Safety Rules
  • Green Light: Proceed only when intersection is clear
  • Yellow Light: Prepare to stop unless unsafe to do so
  • Red Light: Complete stop before crosswalk or stop line
  • Right on Red: Permitted after complete stop and clearance check
  • Flashing Lights: Treat as stop or yield signs accordingly
‚úÖ Stop Sign Procedures
  • Come to complete stop at white stop line or before crosswalk
  • If no line or crosswalk, stop before entering intersection
  • First to stop = first to proceed (if multiple vehicles)
  • Simultaneous stops: rightmost vehicle proceeds first
  • Scan all directions before and during movement

üîÑ Roundabout Navigation

‚úÖ Roundabout Safety Sequence
  • Approach: Reduce speed, choose correct lane for destination
  • Yield: Yield to all traffic already in roundabout
  • Enter: Enter only when safe gap exists, do not stop in roundabout
  • Navigate: Stay in lane, maintain slow but steady speed
  • Exit: Signal right before exit, watch for pedestrians
  • Emergency Vehicles: Continue to exit, then pull over

üåßÔ∏è Adverse Weather Conditions

‚úÖ Weather-Specific Adjustments
  • Rain: Double following distance, watch for hydroplaning
  • Snow/Ice: Triple stopping distance, avoid sudden maneuvers
  • Fog: Use low beams, increase scanning frequency
  • Glare: Use sun visors, polarized sunglasses when needed
  • All Conditions: Increase scanning, reduce speed, signal early
‚ö†Ô∏è Wet Weather Intersection Tips
  • Brake early and gently to avoid skidding
  • Watch for standing water that could hide potholes
  • Increase scanning for pedestrians with umbrellas
  • Be prepared for reduced tire traction on painted lines
  • Allow extra time for turns and lane changes

üöóüöõ Heavy Traffic & Large Vehicles

‚ö†Ô∏è Sharing Intersections with Large Vehicles
  • Never stop beside large trucks in intersections (blind spots)
  • Give trucks extra space for wide turns
  • Watch for turn signals on trailers, not just cabs
  • Be patient - trucks need more time to accelerate and stop
  • Never cut in front of large vehicles at intersections
‚úÖ High-Traffic Intersection Strategies
  • Plan Ahead: Know your route and lane requirements
  • Stay Patient: Avoid aggressive maneuvers and honking
  • Maintain Buffer: Keep space cushions around your vehicle
  • Watch Patterns: Observe traffic flow and light timing
  • Eye Contact: Establish visual contact with other drivers when possible
üéØ Intersection Safety Mastery
  • Scan Constantly: Your eyes should never stop moving at intersections
  • Control Speed: Approach at speeds that allow complete stopping
  • Communicate Clearly: Use signals early and maintain them through turns
  • Respect Right-of-Way: Know when to yield and when to proceed
  • Expect the Unexpected: Always anticipate other drivers' errors
  • Practice Defensive Driving: Your safety depends on your actions, not just others'
