What is Highway Hypnosis? It's not just "daydreaming" - it's a dangerous state of altered consciousness where you drive on "autopilot" without conscious awareness or memory of the journey.

Science Behind It: Your brain filters out repetitive stimuli after 15-20 minutes (called "sensory adaptation"). On highways, this means you stop consciously seeing lane markers, making you vulnerable to hypnosis.

Quick Self-Assessment: Before driving, ask yourself...

B Am I driving alone?

üéØ Recognition: Know the Warning Signs

Learn to recognize symptoms at different stages: Early detection is key to prevention. Match symptoms to their risk level to understand when action is needed.

Mild Warning
Early Stage Signs
  • Mind wandering occasionally
  • Mild boredom setting in
  • Thinking about other things
  • Light daydreaming
Moderate Risk
Progressing Symptoms
  • Can't recall last 5 miles
  • Heavy eyelids, frequent yawning
  • Difficulty focusing on road
  • Feeling unusually sleepy
Severe Danger
Immediate Action Needed
  • Missing exits or turns
  • Drifting between lanes
  • Microsleeps (2-3 second naps)
  • Driving on autopilot
