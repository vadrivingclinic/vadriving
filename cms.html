CMS-1500 Form Completion Learning Module

Version 02-12 | OMB Control Number: 0938-1197

Module Overview

This module provides a comprehensive guide to completing the CMS-1500 claim form (version 02-12), the standard paper form for submitting healthcare claims to Medicare and other insurers when electronic submission is not possible or when using a clearinghouse.

Key Facts: The CMS-1500 form is maintained by the National Uniform Claim Committee (NUCC) and approved by the White House Office of Management and Budget (OMB). Version 02-12 is the current approved version.

Learning Objectives

Objective 1

Identify the purpose and importance of the CMS-1500 form in healthcare billing

Objective 2

Correctly complete patient, insured, and insurance information blocks

Objective 3

Accurately document provider information, services rendered, and diagnoses

Objective 4

Apply proper formatting rules for dates, codes, and identifiers

Objective 5

Recognize when to leave blocks blank or mark them appropriately

Key Concepts & Form Structure

Form Sections

Member Information (Blocks 1-13)

Patient demographics, insurance details, and coordination of benefits

Provider/Supplier Information (Blocks 14-33)

Service details, diagnoses, procedures, and billing information

Tip: Always use an "X" to mark checkboxes. Special characters are not allowed on claim forms.
Important: Medicare only accepts paper claims that use ALL CAPITAL LETTERS.

Detailed Block Instructions

Critical Patient & Insurance Blocks

1 Insurance Type

Check the box indicating what kind of insurance is applicable (Medicare, Medicaid, Tricare, etc.)

1A Medicare HICN

Patient's Medicare Health Insurance Claim Number. Must be recorded whether Medicare is primary or secondary payer.

3 Patient Date of Birth & Sex

Format: MM | DD | CCYY (8-digit format)

Example: September 28, 1990 = 09 | 28 | 1990

11 Primary/Secondary Insurance Determination

Most Important Block: Indicates that a good faith effort has been made to determine whether Medicare is primary or secondary insurance.

When Medicare is Secondary:
  • Group health plan coverage
  • Working aged or disability (large group health plan)
  • End-stage renal disease
  • No-fault or other liability insurance
  • Work-related illness/injury (Workers' Compensation, Black Lung, Veterans benefits)

12 Patient Signature

Authorization for release of medical information. Must be dated (6- or 8-digit format). Signature on file or computer-generated signature is acceptable.

Provider & Service Information

17 Referring/Ordering Provider

Name of referring, ordering, or supervising provider. Use appropriate qualifiers:

  • DN: Referring Provider
  • DK: Ordering Provider
  • DO: Supervising Provider

17B NPI Number

National Provider Identifier - unique identification number for all HIPAA-covered entities.

21 Diagnosis Codes

Enter ICD diagnosis codes. Note: Transition from ICD-9-CM to ICD-10-CM occurred in September 2015.

24 Service Line Details

Multiple sub-blocks for service details:

  • 24A: Dates of service (MMDDYY or MMDDCCYY)
  • 24B: Place of service codes (HIPAA-compliant 2-digit codes)
  • 24D: CPT/HCPCS codes with modifiers
  • 24E: Diagnosis reference code (from Block 21)
  • 24F: Billed charges
  • 24G: Days/units (enter "1" for single service)

Common Scenarios & Special Instructions

Medicare-Specific Requirements

  • Block 8: Always leave blank for Medicare
  • Block 11D: Leave blank for Medicare claims
  • Block 14: For chiropractic services, enter date of initiation of treatment and x-ray date in Block 19 if used
  • Block 15: Leave blank (not required for Medicare)

Medigap/Supplemental Insurance

  • Block 9: Medigap enrollee information if different from patient
  • Block 9A: Policy/group number preceded by MEDIGAP, MG, or MGAP

Block 19: Additional Claim Information

Used for various purposes:

  • Explain modifier 99 (multiple modifiers)
  • Indicate attached reports/documentation
  • Worker's Compensation requirements (state-specific)
  • Payer-specific qualifiers
